Fraumünster

The monastery

Fraumünster is located in the old town of Zurich. This is a reformed church, which was founded in the 9th century. Originally the monastery was called Monasterium Thuricense. The name Fraumünster appeared for the first time in the 14th century. In the church there are the famous Chagall windows, which consist of a five-part window cycle and a rosette. Nowadays it is a landmark of the city and is visited by about 1000 people daily.
