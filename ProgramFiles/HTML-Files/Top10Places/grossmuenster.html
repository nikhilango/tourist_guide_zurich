Grossmünster

Church

The Grossmünster is an Evangelical Protestant church situated in the old town of Zurich. This is a landmark of Zurich and has historical and architectural significance. Here you can find stained glass windows, the Romanesque crypt, choir windows, bronze doors and the cloister Reformation Museum. The church was built on the 12th-century by the order of Charlemagne as a monastery at the exact place where the graves of Felix and Regula, the patrons of the city, were found. You can also visit the Karlstum, one the towers in the church, where you can find a statue of Charlemagne. And can also climb up the 187 stairs to the top of the tower.
